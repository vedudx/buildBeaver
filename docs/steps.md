# 🇨🇦 How to Start a Business in Canada (Step-by-Step)

## 1. Choose a Business Structure

Decide how your business will be legally set up:

- **Sole Proprietorship**
  - Simplest and cheapest
  - You and the business are the same entity
  - Income is reported on your personal taxes

- **Partnership**
  - Two or more owners
  - Shared responsibility and liability

- **Corporation**
  - Separate legal entity
  - More complex and costly
  - Offers liability protection and tax flexibility

---

## 2. Choose and Register a Business Name

- Pick a unique business name
- Check if it’s available
- Register it if required

In Quebec:

- Register through Registraire des entreprises du Québec

Federal incorporation:

- Available via Corporations Canada

> Note: If operating under your personal legal name as a sole proprietor, registration may not be required.

---

## 3. Register for a Business Number (BN)

- Register with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)
- You’ll receive a **Business Number (BN)**

This allows you to open:

- GST/HST account
- Payroll account
- Corporate tax account (if applicable)

---

## 4. Register for Taxes (GST/HST & QST)

- Required if revenue exceeds **$30,000 in 12 months**
- In Quebec, also register for **QST**

Register with:

- CRA (GST/HST)
- Revenu Québec (QST)

---

## 5. Open a Business Bank Account

- Not mandatory for sole proprietors, but recommended
- Required for corporations
- Keeps finances clean and organized

---

## 6. Set Up Accounting

Track:

- Income
- Expenses
- Taxes collected (GST/QST)

Tools:

- QuickBooks
- Wave
- Spreadsheets (for simple setups)

---

## 7. Understand Your Tax Obligations

### Sole Proprietor:

- Report business income on personal tax return

### Corporation:

- File a separate corporate tax return
- More opportunities for tax planning

---

## 8. Obtain Permits and Licenses (if needed)

- Depends on your industry
- Software / freelance work typically requires none
- Regulated industries (e.g. food, construction) may require permits

---

## 9. Consider Business Insurance

Recommended types:

- General liability insurance
- Professional liability (errors & omissions)

---

## 10. Set Up Business Operations

- Create contracts (clients, terms of service)
- Set up invoicing system
- Build or update your website
- Separate personal and business finances

---

## ✅ Quick Start Path (Freelancers / Developers)

1. Start as a sole proprietor
2. Register business name (if not using your own)
3. Track income and expenses
4. Register for GST/QST when approaching $30k revenue
5. Incorporate later if needed

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Incorporating too early
- Mixing personal and business finances
- Not setting aside money for taxes
- Ignoring GST/QST requirements
